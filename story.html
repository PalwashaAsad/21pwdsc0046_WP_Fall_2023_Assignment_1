My Travel Story

Kalam

Kalam is a picturesque valley located in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan. It is nestled in the Swat Valley, surrounded by snow-capped mountains, lush green meadows, and crystal-clear rivers. My travel story of Kalam, KPK begins with my journey from Islamabad to Kalam, which was a scenic drive through the breathtaking landscapes of Swat. As I reached Kalam, I was greeted by the pleasant weather and the warm hospitality of the locals. I checked into a cozy guesthouse and set out to explore the natural beauty of the area. One of the highlights of my trip was visiting the stunning Ushu Forest. The forest was filled with tall cedar trees and offered a serene and tranquil atmosphere. I took long walks amidst the greenery, listening to the sounds of nature and breathing in the fresh mountain air.

Mahudand Lake

Another must-visit place in Kalam was the Mahodand Lake, it is a lake situated in the high Ushu Valley at a distance of 35 km (22 miles) from Kalam in the Swat District of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province in Pakistan. It is accessible through the use of the four-wheeled pressure vehicle and is frequently used for boating and fishing. It was a bumpy jeep ride to reach the lake, but the journey was worth it. As I arrived at the lake, I was mesmerized by its pristine beauty. The turquoise blue waters, surrounded by snow-capped mountains, created a postcard-perfect scene. I took a boat ride on the lake, enjoying the tranquility and the breathtaking views.

Ushu valley

I also explored the nearby Ushu Valley, which is known for its enchanting meadows and gushing waterfalls. I trekked through the lush green fields, crossed wooden bridges over sparkling streams, and marveled at the beauty of nature at every turn. Ushu Forest is famous for its spectacular clouds and rainy weather. Tourist attractions Mahodand Lake are located just 27 kilometers (17 miles) from it. In winter there is a famous Ushu glacier block the sole route that leads to Mahodand Lake and the path is only opened during summer when the glacier’s snow melts and the authorities wash off any snow remaining on the road.

Mingora Bazar

The Mingora Bazaar in Swat was another place I visited, where I immersed myself in the local culture, sampled delicious street food, and bought traditional handicrafts as souvenirs. It is renowned for its natural beauty and well known as a tourist centre. The population of the district is 2,309,570, spread over 5,337 km². Mingora is connected by the N-95 and N-45 highways to Peshawar and Islamabad through Mardan.

Kundol Lake

As I embarked on my journey to Kundol Lake, I was filled with anticipation and excitement. The drive from Kalam to the starting point of the trek was an adventure in itself, with scenic views of the Swat Valley and its majestic landscapes. Once I reached the trailhead, I began my trek towards Kundol Lake.

The trek to Kundol Lake was a moderate one, passing through dense forests, crossing gushing streams, and ascending rocky terrains. The path was surrounded by vibrant wildflowers, adding colors to the verdant surroundings. Along the way, I encountered friendly locals who were more than willing to share stories about the lake and the beauty of the region.

As I approached Kundol Lake, I could hear the sound of water cascading down from the mountains. The sight that awaited me was nothing short of breathtaking. The pristine, turquoise-colored lake was nestled amidst the towering peaks, reflecting their magnificence on its calm surface. I found a peaceful spot by the lakeside and sat there, mesmerized by the beauty and serenity of the place.

I spent hours exploring the area around Kundol Lake, walking along its shores, and immersing myself in the tranquility of the surroundings. The crystal-clear water tempted me to dip my feet in, and the cool breeze refreshed my spirit. I captured the surreal beauty of the lake through my camera lens, trying to preserve the memories forever.

As the day progressed, the sunlight danced on the lake's surface, creating a magical play of colors. I sat there, lost in the moment, appreciating the wonders of nature and feeling grateful for being able to witness such a hidden treasure.

Eventually, it was time to bid farewell to Kundol Lake. The trek back was equally enjoyable, as I retraced my steps through the scenic trails, savoring the sights and sounds of nature. The journey had not only offered me a glimpse of the breathtaking Kundol Lake but had also allowed me to connect with nature and find solace in its embrace.

My visit to Kundol Lake was an unforgettable experience, filled with awe-inspiring beauty and a sense of tranquility. It was a reminder of the wonders that nature holds and the importance of preserving such pristine places. Kundol Lake, with its untouched charm, left an indelible mark on my heart, and I left with a promise to return someday to relive the magic it holds.

In the evenings, I would sit by the riverside, watching the sunset paint the sky with vibrant hues. The sound of the river flowing and the cool breeze added to the peaceful ambiance. I also had the opportunity to interact with the friendly locals, who shared stories about their rich culture and traditions. As my trip to Kalam, KPK came to an end, I felt a sense of gratitude and awe for the natural beauty I had witnessed. The majestic mountains, the lush valleys, and the warm hospitality of the locals had left a lasting impression on me. Kalam, KPK had given me a glimpse of paradise on earth, and I left with cherished memories and a desire to return someday to explore more of its hidden treasures.
